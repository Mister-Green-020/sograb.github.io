Abstract

Recent years have seen soft robotic grippers gain increasing attention due to their ability to robustly grasp soft and fragile objects. However, a commonly available standardised evaluation protocol has not yet been developed to assess the performance of varying soft robotic gripper designs. This work introduces a novel protocol, the Soft Grasping Benchmarking and Evaluation (SoGraB) method, to evaluate grasping quality, which quantifies object deformation by using the DensityAware Chamfer Distance (DCD) between point clouds of soft objects before and after grasping. We validated our protocol in extensive experiments, which involved ranking three FinRay gripper designs with a subset of the EGAD object dataset.
